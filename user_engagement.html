User Engagement Analysis

A deeper look at how viewers interact with trending content — tracking likes, comments, and engagement rates over time and across genres. Based on the US YouTube Trending Video Dataset (Kaggle), spanning 2020 – 2023.

Q1. How Has User Engagement Behavior Changed Over Time?

Average likes and comments per trending video per quarter (2020–2023). Both signals trend upward, with likes growing faster than comments.

Source: US_youtube_trending_data.csv · Quarterly averages of likes and comment_count per video entry.

Q2. Do Videos with Many Likes Also Tend to Have More Comments — or Are They Independent?

Scatter plot of average likes vs. average comments per genre. The dashed regression line suggests a general positive correlation — but genres like News & Politics sit far above the line (high comments relative to likes), indicating partial independence.

Source: US_youtube_trending_data.csv · Each point = one genre category. Regression line added for reference.

Q3. Which Genre Shows the Most Active User Engagement? (Likes + Comments)

Stacked horizontal bar: average likes (red) and average comments (grey) per genre, sorted by total engagement. Music leads by a wide margin.

Source: US_youtube_trending_data.csv · Average per-video likes and comments by category.

Q4. Has Engagement Rate Increased or Decreased Over the Years?

Engagement rate = (likes + comments) / views × 100. The overall trend is upward, with a slight dip in 2022 before recovering in 2023.

Source: US_youtube_trending_data.csv · Annual averages across all trending categories.

Data source: Kaggle – YouTube Trending Video Dataset by rsrishav.
